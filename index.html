Tokyo

Tokyo is the capital of Japan and the most populous metropolis in the world.

Paris

Paris, the capital of France, is a major European city and a global center of art, fashion, and culture.

New York

New York City comprises 5 boroughs where the Hudson River meets the Atlantic Ocean.

London

London, the capital of England and the United Kingdom, is a 21st-century city with Roman history.

Bangkok

Bangkok, the capital of Thailand, is a large city known for its ornate shrines and vibrant street life.

Sydney

Sydney, the capital of New South Wales, is known for its Sydney Opera House on the harbor.

The Most Important Monument

Tokyo Tower

The Tokyo Tower is a famous landmark and symbol of the city of Tokyo.

Eiffel Tower

The Eiffel Tower is a wrought-iron lattice tower in Paris and one of the world's most recognizable structures.

Statue of Liberty

The Statue of Liberty is a global icon of freedom and one of the most famous landmarks in the United States.

Big Ben

Big Ben is the nickname for the Great Bell of the clock at the north end of the Palace of Westminster in London.

Grand Palace

The Grand Palace is a complex of buildings in the heart of Bangkok, Thailand.

Sydney Opera House

The Sydney Opera House is a performing arts center in Sydney and one of the most famous 20th-century buildings.
